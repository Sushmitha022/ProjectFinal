History

Rushikonda Beach holds a layered historical and cultural legacy rooted in the ancient maritime and spiritual traditions of the eastern coast of India. Situated along the Bay of Bengal in Visakhapatnam, the region was once an integral part of the flourishing Kalinga kingdom, known for its strategic coastal trade routes and robust naval prowess. These waters witnessed the exchange of goods, culture, and faith across Southeast Asia, as Kalinga's seafarers voyaged to distant shores. During the medieval and early modern periods, Rushikonda remained relatively undisturbed, known primarily as a quiet fishing village wrapped in dense greenery and surrounded by the Eastern Ghats. The traditional livelihoods of the local communities revolved around fishing, salt-making, and small-scale agriculture. Even as Visakhapatnam began to develop into a strategic port town under colonial rule, Rushikonda retained its serene, untouched quality. It wasn‚Äôt until the late 20th century that Rushikonda began to gain prominence in the public eye. With Visakhapatnam‚Äôs rapid transformation into a bustling industrial, naval, and educational hub, urban sprawl extended along the coast. Recognizing the ecological and touristic value of Rushikonda‚Äôs pristine shores, the Andhra Pradesh Tourism Development Corporation (APTDC) launched efforts to develop the beach into a model for eco-tourism. Safety Measures

  • Water Sports: Always wear a life jacket and follow all safety instructions from certified instructors. Avoid participating during rough sea conditions.
  • Yoga and Meditation: Practice in shaded or designated calm areas. Stay hydrated and avoid mid-day heat during sessions.
  • Kailasagiri Hill Park Visit: Use proper footwear for walking trails and follow park safety signs. Be cautious on hilltop viewpoints.
  • Sunrise Photography: Arrive early for the best light. Stay on safe, stable ground and avoid slippery rocks or ledges.
  • General: Carry sunscreen, water, and avoid entering the sea after sunset. Respect local rules and lifeguard instructions.
