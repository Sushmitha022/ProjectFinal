Bheemunipatnam Beach, also known as Bheemili Beach, is a serene coastal stretch located at the mouth of the Gosthani River, about 25 km from Visakhapatnam, in the town of Bheemunipatnam, Andhra Pradesh. This beach stands out not only for its natural beauty but also for its rich historical and cultural legacy. Lined with palm trees, soft sands, and calm waters, Bheemunipatnam offers a peaceful escape from city life and is perfect for travelers seeking a quiet, reflective seaside experience. Bheemili Beach is far less commercialized than other popular beaches in the region, making it a great spot for long walks, photography, and soaking in the coastal charm. What makes this beach truly unique is its blend of natural landscapes and colonial history, visible through remnants of old Dutch and British structures, cemeteries, and ancient temples scattered around the area.

Safety Measures

  • Climbing the Lighthouse: If accessible, follow official instructions and safety signs. Avoid leaning over the railings, and supervise children closely.
  • Boat Rides: Always wear a life jacket. Use registered boat services and avoid boating during rough weather or after sunset.
  • Exploration: Be cautious while walking through old cemeteries or ruins. Watch your step and avoid entering structurally weak areas.
  • Photography: Sunrise and sunset photography should be done from safe, stable ground. Avoid getting too close to rocky edges or slippery patches.
  • General: Keep the beach clean, stay hydrated, use sunscreen, and be aware of tide timings before walking along the coast.
